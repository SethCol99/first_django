ATOMS

What exactly are they

As Joe Rosen explains in his encyclopedia on physics, atoms are
the smallest entity that can be identified in a chemical element

  • They are much smaller than the wavelength of visible light, and
    therefore, cannot be seen even through the optical telescope,
    an undetailed image of their structure can be seen in an electron microscope
  • Though very difficult to see, their structure has been rather accurately
    by the field of Quantum Physics, a whole other field of physics
